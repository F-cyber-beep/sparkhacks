Overview

Mental health encompasses psychological, emotional, and social well-being. It is about knowing and bearing oneself- apprehending what makes us joyous, building oneself’s relationships, overcoming day-to-day complications, and maintaining a healthy self-sustaining outlook. It also means maintaining a balance in life with all aspects of life. These include equilibrium between social, physical, spiritual, economic, and mental. Mental health affects our sense of thought, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, habituate to certain circumstances, and decipher our actions. Mental health is vital to every stage of life, from childhood to adolescence through adulthood and elderly life. Mental health manifests within us all! Over the course of your life, if you experience mental health problems, your thinking, mood, and behavior could be affected. Many factors contribute to psychological issues, including:

  • Biological factors, such as genes or brain chemistry
  • Life experiences, such as trauma, abuse, anxiety, fluster, etc
  • Family history of mental health problems Mental health problems are common but help is Here.
People with mental health issues can get better and many recover completely. You are not alone. Take action now! Chat with Spark now!

    • In Emergencies:

  • Psychotherapy: is a way to help people with a broad variety of mental illnesses and emotional difficulties by exploring their thoughts, behaviours and emotions. Additionally, it can also help eliminate or control troubling symptoms so a person can recover and improve well-being!
  • Medication: medication can be a vital tool to minimize or withdraw an illness.The best medications for you will depend on your particular situation and how your body responds to the medication.
  • Case Management: Case management coordinates services for an individual with the help of a case manager. A case manager can help assess, plan, and implement a number of strategies to facilitate recovery.
  • Hospitalization: In a minority of cases, hospitalization may be necessary so that an individual can be closely monitored, accurately diagnosed or have medications adjusted when his or her mental illness temporarily worsens.
  • Peer Support: Peer Support refers to receiving help from individuals who have suffered from similar experiences.
